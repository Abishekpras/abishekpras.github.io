METAPHORS IN COMPUTER SCIENCE

Storytelling is an art! A compelling story can engage listeners and plant ideas, emotions and thoughts into their brains. As Richard Dawkins explained in his influential book 'The Selfish Gene', humans are distinct from other animals in their ability to project themselves onto others' experiences, allowing them to learn vicariously. We relate ourselves and our accumulated knowledge with the stories we listen to, and it is these metaphors that help us connect with them.

One of the major turning points in my life was when I came across the field of "Computational Intelligence", which primarily dealt with the problem of learning via nature-inspired computational approaches. I started to appreciate the far-reaching implications of Computer Science, and came to realize how fundamental principles in the field tend to be so general as to be applicable even to larger concepts like life and society! Metaphors in Computer Science is my attempt to share these beautiful examples to help the reader look at research in Computer Science through a whole new perspective.
